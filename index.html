Research Interests

My research sits at the intersection of interaction design and social computing. I study the everyday practices of collaboration and learning, and I build and evaluate tools that make those practices richer, fairer, and more accessible.

Broadly, I am currently drawn to three questions:

Collaborative & social computing

How do groups coordinate, share knowledge, and build trust through technology — and how can design reduce friction and inequity in that process?

Technology-supported learning

Designing interactive systems that support language learning and skill acquisition, with attention to feedback, motivation, and self-directed practice.

Human-centered & responsible AI

Understanding how people interpret and rely on AI-generated support, and designing interfaces that keep humans in meaningful control.
